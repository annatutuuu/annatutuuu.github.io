I'm a Financial Technology graduate student at **Duke University** (MEng, expected May 2027), 
with a B.Sc. in Economics and Mathematics from the **University of St Andrews**, 
and an exchange year at the **University of Virginia**.
My work sits at the intersection of data science and quantitative finance, especially in the 
quantitative risk area — I enjoy turning messy financial data into structured insights that 
support risk and investment decisions.

Previously, I interned at **Fortune SG Fund Management** and **Hwabao Securities**, where I built 
Python pipelines for ETF risk analysis, computed factor exposures across large index datasets, and 
designed dashboards to track portfolio risk metrics. I've worked across equity, fixed income, and 
derivatives contexts, and have hands-on experience with VaR, Expected Shortfall, and multi-factor 
risk modeling.

Outside of finance, I've applied causal inference methods (IPW, standardization) to public health 
data for my Honours dissertation, and contributed to large-scale entity-resolution research as a 
Research Assistant at St Andrews' Business School.

I'm currently seeking **full-time or co-op roles** in quantitative risk or data science within 
financial services, starting Fall 2026.

**Technical skills:** Python (pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn), R, SQL, C++, MATLAB, Excel (advanced)
